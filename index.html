The Mecca of Kendo
in Canada

Created in 1914, Steveston Kendo club was the first ever kendo dojo. originally under the name “Yokikan dojo” until it was closed during world war 2 in 1939. Reopening after the war ended, today, Steveston kendo club is home to over 60 yearly members located at the Steveston martial arts center.

Kendo from many
walks of life

Steveston Kendo club has kendoka of varying ages. from 8 years old to above the age of 60. Along with not only players in the Richmond area, but also players coming from all across the lower mainland. Kendo is a great opportunity for a physical activity that hones in on people looking to improve their discipline, confidence, and spirit.

Beginners Course

The summer introductory kendo course is designed to welcome beginners and build a solid foundation in both the physical and philosophical aspects of the art as well as to provide a short term experience to see if kendo is right for them.
