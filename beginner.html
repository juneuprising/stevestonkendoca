Step into the world of Japanese swordsmanship with our intro to kendo class. This course covers the fundamentals of kendo, including basic footwork, strikes, etiquette, and the philosophy behind the art.

What to expect

Intro summer class registration usually begins in May, with classes beginning in early June. Classes will run on Wednesdays and Fridays from 7:00pm to 8:00pm and will run until the end of August. By the end of the summer beginner course, students should expect to learn basic manners, simple Japanese phrases, foot work and how to swing the shinai.


By the end of their beginner year of kendo, students will hopefully be at the point where they now have a full set of bogu (armor) and be able to perform drills and spar with other students in the kendo club, some may have the opportunity to compete in local tournaments.


MEMBERS WHO ALREADY HAVE BOGU: Please contact us through email to see if you can skip classes

Learning timeline

  • First day: etiquette, bowing
  • 1 Month: swinging shinai, foot work
  • 6 months: hitting men, kote and dou
  • 1 year: hiki waza, kaishi waza
  • 2 years: shiai
