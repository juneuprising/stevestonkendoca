Kendo is a modern Japanese martial art that means “The Way of the Sword.” It is derived from traditional samurai swordsmanship and combines physical training with mental discipline. Practitioners, called kendoka, wear protective armor (bogu) and use bamboo swords (shinai) to practice strikes, footwork, and sparring.
All of the mandatory kendo materials are provided on your first day, however please be ready to provide bring registration confirmation to verify payment Please wear comfortable clothes that you are ready to do physical activity in such as t-shirts, shorts or sweatpants. please do not wear jewellery or watches.
Please do not purchase additional gear such as gi/hakama or bogus until explicitly told by a sensei If you have reached a point where you do need to purchase kendo gear, places like Bogushop are local options that are recommended for beginners to purchase from.
The minimum age at Steveston kendo club is 8 years old. There is no maximum age, however, kendo does have a lot of physical contact, so please keep that in mind when singing up.
Due to protective gear and technique kendo is generally safe from major injuries, especially compared to other contact sports and martial arts. minor injuries like bruises, blisters and sprains are pretty common but major injuries are much more rare. Some major injuries in kendo come from lower leg and shoulder areas. if there is any concern about this please seek a medical professional’s advice if kendo is right for you.
